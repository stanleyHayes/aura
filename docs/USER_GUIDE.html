Introduction

1. What AURA is, and who it’s for

AURA (Ashesi University Resource Allocation) is Ashesi’s space-management platform. It brings the university’s rooms, the live class timetable, maintenance windows and ad-hoc booking requests into one place, so everyone is looking at the same source of truth.

AURA answers one question reliably: “Is this room actually free at this time?” It does this by combining three things — the imported semester timetable (lectures), approved bookings, and scheduled maintenance — and showing only the gaps that remain. Staff can request a room in those gaps, a booking officer reviews the request with full conflict information in front of them, and administrators keep the catalogue, semesters and users in order.

Who uses AURA

  • Lecturers & staff who need a room for a class, seminar, club or event.
  • Booking officers who review and decide on requests.
  • Timetable administrators who load each semester’s schedule.
  • System administrators who run the whole platform.

What it replaces

  • Email chains and spreadsheets for “who has the room?”
  • Double bookings and clashes with lectures.
  • Guesswork about capacity and equipment.
  • Manual record-keeping — every change is logged.

AURA runs on the web (any modern browser) and as a mobile app with the same search, booking, approvals and reports. This guide focuses on the web app; the mobile screens use the same labels.

Getting started

2. Getting started

2.1 Where and how to sign in

Live web address
https://auragh.vercel.app
For a local demo on your own machine, AURA runs at http://localhost:3001 instead. Use the same accounts and steps either way.

If a demo login is rejected on the live site, ask your administrator to create it (or use your own real account).

2.3 Switching light / dark, and choosing a colour tint

AURA has two separate appearance controls:

  1. Light or dark mode — use the theme button in the top bar (the sun/moon icon). It flips the whole interface between light and dark and remembers your choice on that device.
  2. Dark screen tint — go to Settings → Preferences and find the Appearance card. Under “Dark screen tint” pick one of nine colour casts for dark mode. The card notes: “Use the theme button in the top navigation to switch modes. These tints change the dark screen colour only.”

The tint only changes how dark mode looks — light mode stays the same. The nine tints are Ink (the default warm black), Burgundy, Midnight, Canopy, Slate, Ocean, Plum, Espresso and Rose. Full list in Section 6.

2.4 Signing out

Open the account menu (your initials, top-right) and choose Sign out — described as “End your session on this device.” You’re returned to the sign-in page. Always sign out on shared or public computers.

Step D — Track and cancel your bookings

Open My bookings (“Track the status of every request you’ve made.”). Filter tabs across the top are All, Pending, Approved, Rejected, Cancelled and Expired. 4.2 Booking Officer — review & decide

Step A — Open the approvals queue

From the Overview menu choose Approvals. The page is the Approvals queue: “Each request shows exactly why it can — or cannot — be approved, so you can resolve conflicts without guessing.” Three stat cards at the top show Pending requests, Approved requests and Rejected requests.

Step B — Read why a request is approvable or blocked

Each request card shows the room, the purpose, the requester (and department), and tiles for Date, Time, Attendees and Requested by. A badge reads either Approvable or Blocked:

  • Approvable → a Ready for approval panel, e.g. “No lecture, maintenance, capacity, or booking conflicts were detected.”
  • Blocked → a Needs attention before approval panel listing the reasons. Each blocker is one of these:

Step C — Approve, reject, or override

  1. Approve — available only when the badge says Approvable. Click it; you’ll see “Booking approved.”
  2. Reject — opens the Reject this request dialog. A Reason is required (“The requester will be notified with your reason”) — e.g. “The room is reserved for an examination that week.” Click Reject request.
  3. Override — only for System Admins with override rights, and only on blocked requests. The Override and approve dialog warns “This bypasses normal conflict checks” and requires a Justification. You can also tick “Cancel any conflicting approved bookings and notify those requesters.” The action is recorded in the audit log.

Step D — View utilisation & booking reports

Choose Reports (“Utilisation, bookings and conflicts. Export large datasets to CSV, Excel or PDF.”). Set the From / To date range (defaults to the last 30 days), then use the three tabs:

  • Utilisation — a Room utilisation bar chart plus a table (Room, Capacity, Lecture h, Booked h, Utilisation). Export CSV downloads it.
  • Bookings — metric cards Total requests, Approved, Rejected, Approval rate, and a Bookings by department chart.
  • Conflicts — metric cards Rejected requests, Cancelled bookings, Expired requests.

4.3 Timetable Administrator — import a semester timetable

This is the most important admin workflow. The exact column rules live in Section 5.1; this is the click-by-click procedure.

Before you start

Make sure the semester exists (a System Admin creates it under Semesters). Then choose Timetable from the Scheduling menu. The page is Timetable import: “Upload the semester schedule from Excel or CSV. Replacing a timetable never touches existing bookings.”

  1. Pick the Semester the file belongs to (a searchable list).
  2. Choose the Mode: Append (add to what’s there) or Replace (“clears existing lecture events for the semester first”). Either way, existing bookings are never touched.
  3. Attach your File (.xlsx or .csv). Not sure of the format? Click Download CSV template for a ready-made starter file (aura-timetable-template.csv).
  4. Decide on Create missing rooms & buildings (on by default). When on, a Location naming an unknown room is added automatically — see Section 5.1. Turn it off to reject unknown rooms as errors instead.
  5. Click Upload timetable. The Import progress panel shows a live percentage and three counters: Total rows, Imported, and Needs review.
  6. If any rows fail, read the Row error report (Row / Field / Message). Fix those rows in your source file and upload again. Imported rows are already live.

4.4 System Administrator — run the platform

The System Administrator can reach everything below. Each item is its own sidebar entry.

Manage users

Open Users (“Manage accounts, roles and access across the system.”).

  1. Add a user — click Invite user. Fill Full name and Email (both required), pick a Role (defaults to Requester), optionally set a Department, then Invite user.
  2. Change a role — on any row open the Manage menu → Change role → choose System administrator, Timetable administrator, Booking officer or Requester.
  3. Suspend / reactivate — from the same Manage menu, Suspend account (for active users) or Reactivate account. Status badges read active, suspended or pending verification.

Manage rooms, buildings & equipment

Under the Catalogue menu:

  • Rooms — search by name, code or building; filter by Room type and Status. New room opens a form (Room code, Capacity, Name, Building, Room type) — save with Save room. Row actions: View, Edit, Deactivate.
  • Buildings — fields Code, Name, optional Campus. New building / Save.
  • Equipment — fields Code (e.g. PROJECTOR) and Name (e.g. Projector). New equipment / Save.

On any item’s detail page, Add images opens an uploader with a Main image and a Gallery — see Section 5.3. Each catalogue screen also has an Import button for bulk CSV/TSV/JSON — see Section 5.2.

Create & activate semesters

Open Semesters (“Only the active semester affects availability.”). Click New semester, enter Name (e.g. 2026/27 Semester 1), Start date and End date, then Create. New semesters are draft. Use the row buttons Activate (makes it the live semester) and Archive.

Schedule maintenance windows

Open Maintenance (“Blocked periods never appear as available and cannot be booked.”). Click New window, choose the Room (required), set Date, From, To, and a Reason (required, e.g. Projector replacement), then Create. Remove a window with its Remove button.

Override bookings, reports & the audit log

  • Override bookings — on the Approvals queue, blocked requests show an Override button (Section 4.2, Step C).
  • Reports — same Utilisation / Bookings / Conflicts tabs and CSV/Excel/PDF export as the officer (Section 4.2, Step D).
  • Audit log — “An append-only record of every state change. Read-only.” Columns: When, Actor, Action, Entity, IP. Filter with “Filter by entity type (e.g. booking).”

5.1 Timetable import (the main one)

This is the importer that powers availability — its lecture rows are what AURA treats as “the room is in use”. Document it carefully.

Accepted files & limits

  • An .xlsx Excel workbook (only the first worksheet is read) or a .csv file.
  • Up to 20,000 rows. Header matching is case-insensitive, and surrounding spaces are ignored.
  • Blank rows are skipped. Columns AURA doesn’t recognise are simply ignored.

Required columns (any one accepted name per column)

Timetable — required columns
ColumnAccepted header namesFormat / rule
Course CodeCourse CodeAny text, e.g. CS101. Cannot be blank.
Course TitleCourse Title · Course NameAny text.
LecturerLecturer · Lecturer Name · Staff NameAny text.
RoomRoom · LocationMust identify a room (see “How a room is matched” below).
DayDayMon or Monday (Mon–Sun).
Start TimeStart Time · From Time24-hour HH:MM (e.g. 08:00) or h:mm AM/PM.
End TimeEnd Time · To TimeSame time formats; must be after Start Time.

If any required column is missing entirely, the whole upload is rejected before processing with a message naming the missing column and the names it accepts.

Optional columns (captured when present)

Timetable — optional columns
ColumnAccepted namesWhat it’s used for
SectionSectionStored on the lecture event.
ProgramProgramStored on the lecture event.
DepartmentDepartmentStored on the lecture event.
Number of EnrollmentsNumber of Enrollments · EnrollmentsSeeds the capacity of any new room AURA creates for this row.

How a room is matched (and the “Create missing” toggle)

For each row, AURA finds the room by trying, in order: (a) an exact room code, then (b) an exact room name. If neither matches:

  • “Create missing rooms & buildings” ON (the default) — AURA splits the Location into a building and a room number and creates them. The last word is the room number; the rest is the building. So Nutor Hall 115 → building Nutor Hall + room 115. New rooms are created as Lecture halls, with capacity taken from Number of Enrollments (or 30 if absent).
  • Toggle OFF — an unknown room becomes a row error (“unknown room”); that row is skipped, the rest still import.

Append vs Replace

ModeEffect
AppendAdds the file’s rows to the chosen semester’s existing lecture events.
ReplaceClears that semester’s lecture events first, then imports the file.

Bookings are never touched by either mode — Replace only clears timetabled lecture events, not approved bookings or maintenance. This is a core safety rule of AURA.

Other per-row rules

  • End time must be after start time, or the row errors out.
  • Two rows describing the same room + day + exact times are flagged as a “duplicate of an earlier row in this upload.”
  • An import finishes as Completed (all rows in), Partially completed (some row errors), or Failed (nothing imported).

5.2 Catalogue import (rooms, buildings & equipment)

Each catalogue screen (Rooms, Buildings, Equipment) has an Import button that opens a bulk importer. The dialog says “CSV, TSV or JSON. Expected columns: …” and lists the columns for that type. It matches existing items by code and updates them, or creates them if new. Column names are case-insensitive and spaces or hyphens are treated as underscores.

Catalogue import — required & accepted columns
ImportingRequired columnsAlso accepted / optional
Buildings code, name campus (optional). Aliases: building_code, building_name.
Equipment code, name Aliases: equipment_code, equipment_name.
Rooms room_code, name, building_code, capacity, room_type, status Aliases: code, room_name, building / building_name / building_id, seats, type.

Rules for the Rooms import specifically:

  • Building must match an existing building’s code, name or id (rooms aren’t auto-created here — create the building first).
  • Capacity must be a whole number of at least 1.
  • room_type is one of LECTURE_HALL, LAB, SEMINAR_ROOM, AUDITORIUM, CONFERENCE_ROOM (defaults to LECTURE_HALL if blank).
  • status is one of ACTIVE, INACTIVE, UNDER_MAINTENANCE (defaults to ACTIVE if blank).

After running, a summary shows {n} imported, {n} errors and {n} rows read, with a Rows that need attention list naming each bad row and why. Row numbers start at 2 (row 1 is the header).

Quick CSV examples:
Buildings → code,name,campus
Equipment → code,name
Rooms → room_code,name,building_code,capacity,room_type,status

5.3 Image uploads (rooms, buildings & equipment photos)

On a room, building or equipment detail page, click Add images. The picker offers a single Main image and a multi-image Gallery (“Main image and gallery are uploaded from this device”). Choose your files, then Upload images.

  • Accepted types: JPEG, PNG, WebP or GIF only.
  • Up to 12 gallery images per upload, plus the one main image.
  • Each upload is capped at 40 MB total.

This is a media upload, not a data import — there are no “columns.” It’s listed here so the picture is complete: aside from images, the timetable and catalogue importers above are the only file imports in AURA.

6. Settings in depth

Open Settings from the sidebar or the account menu (“Manage your profile, security, notifications and preferences.”). It has four tabs: Profile, Security, Notifications, Preferences.

Profile

The Edit profile card lets you keep your details current: Full name (required), a read-only Email (“managed by your administrator”), and an optional Department. Save with Save profile. The right-hand panel summarises your identity, account status, MFA state, department and last sign-in.

Security — change password

The Password card asks for your Current password, a New password and Confirm password, then Update password.

Security — enable two-factor authentication (MFA)

The Multi-factor authentication card (“Protect sign-in with a six-digit authenticator code”) shows your status as Enabled or Not enabled. To turn it on:

  1. Click Enable MFA. AURA shows a QR code and the heading “Add AURA to an authenticator app.”
  2. Scan the QR code with Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator, 1Password, or another TOTP app. (If you can’t scan, use Open authenticator setup, or copy the Manual setup secret with the Copy button.)
  3. Enter the six-digit code from the app into the “Enter six-digit code” box and click Verify and enable.
  4. You’re done — “Your next sign-in will ask for an authenticator code.”

Notifications

The Notifications card (“Choose how AURA alerts you in this browser”) has an In-app alerts toggle (“Show booking and approval alerts in the top navigation”). Click Save notifications to keep your choice.

Preferences — appearance & theme

The Preferences card offers Spoken guide and Compact tables toggles (Save preferences). Below it, the Appearance card sets your Dark screen tint. Remember: the light/dark switch itself is the theme button in the top bar; these tints only recolour dark mode.

Good to know

7. Tips & good-to-know

Times are local. Every date and time in AURA is West Africa Time (Africa/Accra). You don’t need to convert anything.
There’s a mobile app. Search, book, approvals and reports work on mobile too, with the same light/dark themes and labels.
Only the active semester counts. Availability is driven by the one Active semester’s timetable — so activate the right semester before people start searching.
A room must be free for the whole window. If a room only shows part of your time as free, narrow your From / To to that gap.
Replace is safe. Re-importing a timetable in Replace mode never deletes anyone’s bookings — it only refreshes the lecture rows.
Cancel is instant. There’s no confirmation prompt when you cancel a booking — double-check before clicking.

7.1 Glossary

Requester
Anyone who searches for and requests a room (typically a lecturer or staff member).
Booking Officer
Reviews pending requests and approves or rejects them.
Timetable Administrator
Imports each semester’s class schedule.
System Administrator
Runs the platform — users, catalogue, semesters, maintenance, overrides, reports, audit.
Semester
A teaching period with a name and dates. Status is Draft, Active or Archived; only the Active one drives availability.

Override
An admin forcing a booking through despite conflicts — always logged in the audit trail.

An append-only, read-only history of every change in AURA.

7.2 Frequently asked questions

I searched but no rooms appeared — why?

Usually the rooms are occupied for part of your window (a room must be free for the entire From–To range), or your capacity / equipment filters are too strict. Widen the window, lower the minimum capacity, or remove some equipment requirements. Also confirm a semester is Active.

How long until my request is approved?

A booking officer reviews requests in the Approvals queue. You’ll get an in-app notification when a decision is made; track it under My bookings.

Can I undo a cancellation?

No. Cancelling is immediate and there’s no confirmation step, so check the details first. If you still need the room, submit a fresh request.

My timetable upload says “Partially completed.”

Some rows passed and some failed. Open the Row error report (Row / Field / Message), fix only those rows in your source file, and upload again. Imported rows are already live.

The importer can’t find my room.

Either the Location doesn’t match an existing room code/name and Create missing rooms & buildings is off, or the spelling differs. Turn the toggle on to auto-create it, or correct the Location to match the catalogue.

How do I change someone’s role or disable an account?

A System Administrator opens Users, uses the row Manage menu to Change role, and Suspend account / Reactivate account as needed.

I forgot my password.

For demo accounts the shared password is provided on request. For a real account, change it under Settings → Security, or ask an administrator to reset it.
